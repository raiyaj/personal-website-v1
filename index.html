About

Hello! My name is Raiya. I'm a second-year health sciences student, currently studying at Simon Fraser University in Burnaby, British Columbia. My coding journey began only a short time ago, when I decided to self-learn how to build websites using HTML, CSS and JavaScript, and I soon realized that I had found my calling.

I'm passionate about public health, health informatics, environmental sustainability and, on the other end of the spectrum, about programming and web development. Ultimately, I hope to navigate into a career that lies somewhere at the intersection of health and technology.

Mount Baker

During summer 2013, I partcipated in an inspiring and exhilarating twelve-day science and mountaineering expedition on Mt. Baker, Washington, with the wilderness educational organization Girls on Ice. In between exploring the glacier surrounding our basecamp and learning how to self-arrest with ice axes, my team summited Sherman Peak and carried out a field science study investigating the mountain's alpine ecosystem. Later, we presented our findings at the North Cascades Environmental Learning Center in Rockport, WA.
