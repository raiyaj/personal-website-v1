As a summer student in the Mattsson lab in SFU's Department of Biological Sciences, I cloned various candidate genes and purified plasmids harbouring recombinant DNA in preparation for transformation of rice cell cultures. The goal of this project is to identify transcription factors that have an effect on gene expression and/or vein density in order to minimize photorespiration and maximize photosynthetic yield of rice plants.

Humanitarians in Training is an annual conference hosted by the Red Cross Student Movement, a collaboration between SFU and UBC Red Cross. I designed an issues-based workshop about the challenges countries might face in achieving justice and reconciliaiton after major conflict or human rights violations, then facilitated the workshop at our 2016 conference, held on February 13, at the Abdul Ladha Centre, UBC.

In the summer of 2015, I became involved with Byte Camp, a non-profit dedicated to providing creative technology summer camps for youth across the Lower Mainland, Vancouver Island, and Calgary. As an instructor at various camps, I led hands-on workshops relating to claymation, 3D animation, and 3D video game design using easily-accessible software.

In 2013, I partcipated in an inspiring and exhilarating twelve-day science and mountaineering expedition on Mt. Baker, Washington, with the wilderness educational organization Girls on Ice. In between exploring the glacier surrounding our basecamp, my team summited Sherman Peak and carried out a field science study investigating the mountain's alpine ecosystem. Later, we presented our findings at the North Cascades Environmental Learning Center.
